## a Chord Strum Thingie

<video autoplay loop muted playsinline poster="../assets/images/strumthingie-poster.webp" style="width:640px;max-width:100%"><source src="../assets/images/strumthingie.mp4" type="video/mp4"></video>

A little sound-toy for playing chords with your hands. Pick a chord (one hand sets the root
on the keybed, a couple of buttons stack the flavour: major, minor, 7th, 9th) and then sweep
the rainbow **SONIC STRINGS** to strum it, like running a thumb across an autoharp. It isn't
trying to be an exact clone of the Suzuki Omnichord; it's its own thing.

### How it works

A one-octave keybed along the bottom picks the **root** and fires the chord. One row of
buttons picks the chord **type**, another toggles **modifiers** that stack freely, and a
**voicing** dial cascades the whole chord up and down through inversions and octave-spreads.
Turn it while a chord rings and you hear the chord re-voice live. Above the keys sits the
rainbow strum plate: drag across it, with a mouse or your fingers, and the chord plays as a
harp glissando.

### It picks up a band

The fun part is the **flavour** chip. Leave it neutral and it's a plain chord toy; flip it to
**BOSSA**, **YACHT** or **CITYPOP** and a whole backing personality comes along. The comping
rhythm, the voicing, the timbre and the groove all change together, so the little band follows
your hands into that genre. There's a drum machine and a real-time chord looper under the hood
too, so you can jam over yourself.

It shares its guts with the other dreamengine instruments, the same synth voices and the same
sound engine, so nothing quietly drifts apart. I'm still learning to *play* the thing myself,
and it's very much still in development, but it was already too much fun not to show.
