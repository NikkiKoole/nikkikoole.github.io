Tiny Jam: Pocket Music Toys

A growing collection of pocket-sized music toys — a groovebox, a synth, a drum machine — that fit in your pocket and start making sound the second you open them. Free on iPhone and iPad, with a few optional in-app purchases. No ads, no tracking.

Get in touch

Something's broken, or you have a request? Email nikkikoole@gmail.com.

It's one person — me, Nikki — so you'll reach the developer directly. I read every message.

Frequently asked

How do I unlock an instrument? Tap a locked rack, then "unlock" — it's a one-time in-app purchase. Some instruments are free to play without any purchase at all.

I bought a rack (or the Master Pass) and it's not showing up. Tap Restore Purchases in the app — that re-checks your Apple account and re-enables anything you've already bought. If it still doesn't show up, email me at nikkikoole@gmail.com and I'll sort it out.

What can I buy?

  • Acid Rack ($2.99) — unlocks the acid rack (two 303s, a 909, an 808).
  • E-Piano ($2.99) — unlocks a warm electric-piano keybed.
  • Master Pass ($5.00) — unlocks every rack, now and future.

Is my data collected? No. No ads, no tracking, no accounts.

Mipolai, Nikki Koole

Mipolai, mipolai? What kind of name is that even?

When I was about 4 I had a friend called Nikolai, and my name is Nikki, so together we were Nikki & Nikolai. Except I didn't pronounce it like that, nor register it like that. He was Mipolai, and I was Nikki — pronounced as Nitti.

And when I was thinking about what to call this little studio, I remembered this, and how Mipolai was the best name ever, according to myself at 4 years old.
